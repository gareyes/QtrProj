S.Y. 2021-2022: What To Expect

As of the time this page was updated, most students are halfway through the school year 2021-2022. With the opening date on September 13, the majority of schools continued their distance learning curriculum and system from last year. However, on November 15, 2021, 100 public schools participated in the pilot run of face-to-face classes. These schools were in areas deemed to be low-risk.

It's now January 2022, and COVID-19 cases have surged yet again. Along with the arrival of the new variant, Omicron, health protocols are implemented more strictly. Many schools and universities in Manila have announced class suspensions in lieu of this new surge. As of January 9, a new all-time high of COVID-19 cases has been reached: almost 30,000 infections. Just when face-to-face classes were supposed to expand, this problem steps in and prevents that.

Now, what do we expect to happen? Well, most were already resigned to another whole year of distance learning; for now, the students who were granted limited face-to-face classes will have stay at home amidst the new surge. About half the population has now received their firt dose and waiting for the second; 45%, meanwhile, are fully vaccinated people that are about to receive booster shots. Alert level 4 can possibly be raised in Manila, as unvaccinated people are urged to stay in their homes. It's flu season; confusion to whether one has the simple cold or COVID is more common. The best we could do now is lessen outside exposure, even if one is vaccinated. It's too early in the year to know, but hopefully, a safe return to face-to-face classes will happen by the time next school year comes.
