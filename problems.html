Problems Encountered in Distance Learning

The unexpected arrival of the pandemic has put the whole world into upheaval. While the situation has stabilized and things aren't chaotic as it was before, problems still appear as troublesome as ever. In this page, obstacles encountered by students and teachers alike in distance learning will be discussed.

Connectivity Issues

The Philippines isn't a nation that is known for high internet speeds; connectivity problems are probably the most common type when it comes to online learning. Faulty internet creates problems like unability to attend video conferences, submit assignments, and message teachers and classmates. Learning is rocky if internet is unstable, which only leads to frustration and confusion. Extreme cases of this exists when students travel far just to get somewhere with (limited) signal; this is why it's important for face-to-face to come back as soon as possible, as not all students can afford online learning.

Devices & Gadgets

Another online learning problem is the issue with devices. For one, they aren't exactly affordable, even more so when there is more than one learner in the family. In combination with internet fees, gadgets are simply too expensive for the average family. Aside from that, devices are constantly being upgraded; a model won't be the fastest of its kind in only a span of a year. Specifications of devices show what it's suited to do; outdated ones will have a harder time doing tasks. Getting a new devices goes back to the first problem, which is the budget.

Erroneous Modules

The biggest problem when it comes to modular learning is the errors found in the modules. Over the past year, photos of wrong information found in learners' modules are posted on social media. Horrible grammar mistakes, wrong math equations, and gender stereotypes are some of the things found by the guardians on the childrens' modules. Short Attention Span

While this may not be a physical obstacle, it still undoubtedly affects most people. The shift from a proper learning environment into a home setup has put off our comfortable routine, which affects our focus and concentration. People aren't used to doing work at home, so this makes them rather lazy and prone to procrastination. Distractions in the form of social media, the comfort of a bed, household chores, and others keep people from fully immersing themselves in work. Discipline is especially important, if we want to be productive

Mental & Emotional Health

Aside from the physical health risks brought about by the pandemic, people's minds and emotions are affected, too. Compared to pre-pandemic times, people were allowed to socialize as much as they want; now, limited interactions allowed among peers and teachers. Humans are social creatures, so the sudden lack of socialization may affect mental and emotional health. The distance learning setup may also affect our wellbeing; heavy workloads, prolonged times in front of screens and sitting down, and the stress of what's happening around us may cause mental health to deteriorate. This is why it's important to find hobbies and other positive things to keep us sane during the pandemic.
